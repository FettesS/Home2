Overview of Parts

The list of parts is as follows :

  • Central Processing Unit (CPU)
  • Motherboard
  • Fan
  • Power Supply
  • Random Access Memory (RAM)
  • DVD/Read Only Memory (ROM) Drive
  • Heatsink
  • Hard Drive
  • Universal Serial Bus (USB) Card
  • Expansion Slots

System Unit

This is what the case of the system unit looked like before the group started to dissassemble it.

Central processing unit (CPU)

This is the central processing unit (CPU). The CPU gives instructions to the other parts of the computer. The CPU's job is to carry out commands. The CPU controls the execution of a computer and performs arithmetic operations as well.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board inside a computer. It is a thin square plate that holds many parts including the CPU, memory cards, expansion slots, power connectors, connections and microchips used to power your PC. It also uses expansion cards to control video and audio. The motherboard essentially connects all the parts of the computer indirectly and directly. Its job is to keep everything connected.

Fan

The fan helps to keep the computer cool. This prevents the parts of the computer from overheating. It does this by allowing cool air to come into the system unit, while pushing out hot air from the system unit.

Power Supply

The power supply converts the power from the wall outlet to the type of power needed by the computer. It sends this power through connecting cables to the motherboard and other components.The power supply is where the energy for the entire computer comes from. It also regulates the amount of voltage going into the computer. This ensures that the computer does not get overloaded with electricity.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

RAM stands for Random Access Memory. The RAM is your system short term memory as it only provides a temporary storage of information. It is located on the motherboard. The RAM is an example of secondary storage. The more Random Access Memory you have, the more things your computer can do at the same time. It disappears when you shut off your computer. When working on your document, spreadsheet, powerpoint you need to save it to avoid losing it. When you save it, the RAM connects with your hard drive so it can save your work. It is measured in megabytes and gigabytes.

CD/ROM Drive

ROM stands for Read-Only Memory. It is an example of primary storage. The job of the CD ROM is to store data in form of text and graphics. There are two different sectors are defined in this piece of electronic device: mode 1 and 2. Mode 1 is for storing computer data. Mode 2 is for storing is for computer audio, video, and graphic. Our headphones and speakers can be inserted on the outer part of the CD ROM drive to listen to music.

Heatsink

Since the CPU does a large number of tasks, it needs something to help keep it cool. The heatsink does this job. The heatsink sits on top of the CPU to help get rid of the extra heat that it creates.

Hard Drive

The hard drive is where the long-term storage space for the computer is found. All files, software and documents saved on the computer are stored here. The faster the hard drive, the faster the computer can start programs.
